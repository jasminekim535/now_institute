Profile

Thom Mayne is the founding design director of Morphosis and a Distinguished Professor at UCLA. He received a Pritzker Architecture Prize in 2005, and his work has been widely published and exhibited internationally.

His practice, Morphosis, was founded in 1972 and is now a globally recognized architecture and urban design firm. Morphosis has completed buildings in virtually every building type – from single family housing to large civic institutions. The firm has received six American Institute of Architects (AIA) National Honor Awards, twenty-five AIA California Council Honor Awards, and a host of other international prizes and recognitions.

Mayne has been highly influential in shaping architectural education through his involvement with the Southern California Institute of Architecture (SCI-Arc), which he co-founded in 1972, and through his long tenure at UCLA. His academic work has culminated in the creation of the SUPRASTUDIO at UCLA, a cross-disciplinary program of research studios that engages professional, governmental, and academic partners to research urgent urban conditions worldwide. The Now Institute extends this work through long-term research initiatives in cities across the world.

He was appointed to the United States Commission of Fine Arts by President Obama in 2009 and served as a member of the President's Committee on the Arts and Humanities.
